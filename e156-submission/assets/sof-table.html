GRADE Summary of Findings Table Generator

Generates GRADE-compliant Summary of Findings (SoF) tables as used in Cochrane systematic reviews. Each row represents one outcome with GRADE certainty assessment and absolute/relative effects.

GRADE Framework

The GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation) approach rates the certainty of evidence for each outcome across five domains:

  1. Risk of Bias: Limitations in study design or execution
  2. Inconsistency: Unexplained heterogeneity across studies
  3. Indirectness: Differences between the evidence and the clinical question
  4. Imprecision: Wide confidence intervals or insufficient sample size
  5. Publication Bias: Selective reporting of studies

For observational studies, certainty can be upgraded for: large effect, dose-response, or plausible confounding that would reduce the effect.

Certainty Levels

  • HIGH (4 circles): Very confident the true effect lies close to the estimate
  • MODERATE (3 circles): Moderately confident; true effect is likely close
  • LOW (2 circles): Limited confidence; true effect may be substantially different
  • VERY LOW (1 circle): Very little confidence in the effect estimate
